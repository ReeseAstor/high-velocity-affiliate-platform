Smart plugs are the easiest entry point into home automation. Plug one in, connect it to Alexa, and suddenly you can voice-control any device in your home — lamps, fans, coffee makers, holiday lights, you name it.

But not all smart plugs are created equal. After testing 12 different models over the past month, here are our top recommendations for Alexa users in 2026.

## 1. TP-Link Kasa Smart Plug Mini EP10 — Best Overall ($10.99)

The [TP-Link Kasa EP10](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B091FXLMS8?tag=88away0d-20) is our number one recommendation for good reason: it's small enough that it won't block the second outlet, setup takes under two minutes via the Kasa app, and it works reliably with Alexa every single time.

It supports scheduling, countdown timers, and away mode (which randomly turns devices on and off to simulate presence while you're traveling). At under $11, you can outfit your entire house without breaking the bank.

**Best for:** Anyone who wants a reliable, affordable smart plug that just works.

## 2. Amazon Smart Plug — Best Alexa Integration ($24.99)

The [Amazon Smart Plug](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B089DR29T6?tag=88away0d-20) is the most seamless option if you want zero-friction Alexa setup. It doesn't require a separate app — you set it up entirely through the Alexa app, and it supports Alexa routines natively.

Say "Alexa, goodnight" and it can turn off every connected plug in your home. The integration with Alexa Hunches also means it can learn your patterns and suggest automations.

**Best for:** People who want the simplest possible setup with no extra apps.

## 3. Wyze Plug — Best Budget Option ($7.99)

The [Wyze Plug](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B09VF5R4F7?tag=88away0d-20) is the cheapest smart plug that we'd actually recommend. At $7.99, it supports Alexa, Google Home, and IFTTT, plus it includes scheduling and vacation mode. The Wyze app is well-designed and gives you usage statistics.

**Best for:** Budget-conscious buyers who want reliable smart plug functionality at the lowest price.

## 4. TP-Link Kasa KP125M — Best with Energy Monitoring ($17.99)

If you want to track how much power your devices are consuming, the [Kasa KP125M](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0BPY8GDJB?tag=88away0d-20) adds energy monitoring to the excellent Kasa platform. The app shows real-time wattage, daily and monthly usage, and estimated costs. It also supports Matter, the new smart home standard, for future-proof compatibility.

**Best for:** Anyone who wants to monitor and reduce their electricity usage.

## What to Look For in a Smart Plug

When choosing a smart plug for Alexa, the key factors are:

- **Reliability** — Does it stay connected to your network?
- **Size** — Does it block the second outlet?
- **App quality** — Is scheduling and automation easy to set up?
- **Price** — You'll likely want several, so per-unit cost matters.

All four of our picks check these boxes. The **[TP-Link Kasa EP10](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B091FXLMS8?tag=88away0d-20)** is our top pick for the best combination of reliability, features, and value.
